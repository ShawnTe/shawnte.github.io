Other Cities

Street art is for everyone

While traveling the world in my location independent status over the last few years, I've come to love exploring a city through its street art.

Finding maps through Google that people (or the artists themselves) have created, I've had the opportunity to explore neighborhoods far from the tourist book.

The following images are all from my current hometown of Oakland, CA.
I've taken all the photos myself.

But first, a few words from the notorious mystery man himself, Banksy.

"Graffiti is one of the few tools you have if you have almost nothing. And even if you don't come up with a picture to cure world poverty you can make someone smile while they're having a piss."
Banksy, Banging Your Head Against a Brick Wall

Here's the Global Street Art site based in London.
My thoughts on this site:

  • it's image based, which is awesome, especially given the subject matter
  • there are a ton of images on the home page which can be kinda overwhelming
  • I like how on the images you can tell whether it's a single image or if there's a link through to an album of images
  • I also like how you can link through to the artist from the large home page thumbnail

Overall, the site has a clean look and is easy to navigate.
